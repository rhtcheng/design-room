Productivity in the Workspace

The unsolved question

Productivity, in definition, is measured in terms of the rate of output per unit of input. In the creative industry, productivity comes a long way in terms of input, as producing both functional and aesthetically pleasing work can be a demanding process. Different aspects of the work environment can hinder productivity, thus resulting to employees overworking, wasting valuable time, developing poor mental health, and having a lack of creativity.

Scenario

David is CEO of ABC design, a multidiscipline design firm with around 50 employees, ranging from designers to developers. Lately David has been noticing a decrease in productivity in his studio. His employees seem to be less energetic and enthusiastic. Their productivity levels have been low recently and thus, has affected the quality of work and wellbeing of the employees.

Taking matters into his own hands, David reached out to his friends from other design firm. He asked them how they maximize productivity while keeping in mind the wellbeing of their employees.

Conclusion

In conclusion, small changes can be implemented in order to reach full productivity in a creative environment. After analyzing the atmosphere in smaller studios and larger companies, the various criteria such as dynamics, approaches, and environment, factors ultimately affects their productivity output, which will effectively work depending on the type of environment. However even with the different sizes and criteria, we have come to a conclusion where innovation, time management, collaboration and communication are key elements in which these different environments share in order to create successful work.
